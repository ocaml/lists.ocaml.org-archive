I agree to adopt this governance document. Apologies for my delayed reply,
and thanks to Amir for all the hard work on this.

I partially agree with Fabrice that organizations could be taken into
account, but think the "named" maintainers should be individuals. As Anil
states, it is important to know which individuals specifically have the
rights discussed in this document. I wouldn't be very comfortable knowing
only that "someone" at Jane Street or OCaml Labs or wherever is the one
making decisions. A simple compromise could be to list an individual's
affiliation, e.g.

- Louis Gesbert (OCamlPro)

This would indicate that Louis's involvement is on behalf of OCamlPro, if
that's indeed how he views is involvement.


> Finally, the compiler/distribution is now distributed on ocaml.org, since
caml.inria.fr is deprecated. But is it an ocaml.org project ?

No. There is a variety of content hosted on ocaml.org that is not part of
the "ocaml.org project". It is just the hosting of the content, not the
content itself, that is part of the project. For example, we host slides
from the OCaml meeting but the license clearly indicates that copyright is
maintained by the contributing author.

> What rights have the core-team members over the corresponding pages ?
Shall they care about changing them in the event of the migration of
sources from SVN to GIT, or is it a task for the ocaml.org project itself ?

They have the right suggest changes or submit a pull request for desired
changes (just like anyone in the world). The ocaml.org project admins are
obligated to consider their request and accept if it is in the best
interest of the community. Your example would obviously qualify.

Hope that helps clarify some of the points.

> There is no legal meaning to signing this document, because nobody is
> signing anything.  It is, as Amir noted, a "living document" that
> simply documents what superstructure exists at the moment.  As Xavier
> notes, many of the resources currently used by ocaml.org are owned
> personally by several of us, and we are keen to 1) ensure that their
> ownership is tracked; and 2) a path of authority exists to one person
> (Xavier Leroy) to facilitate any conflict resolution.
>
> There may be an OCaml Foundation in the future, but there have been
> no concrete movements in this direction yet beyond this effort to
> document what currently exists.
>
> > Let's take some examples of things that could also be clarified: if
> > somebody wants a new project to become an ocaml.org project, how long
